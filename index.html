Points System

Every point counts in the race to become Mini QPL Champions

Points Allocation

  • 1st Place 50 points
  • 2nd Place 40 points
  • 3rd Place 30 points
  • 4th Place 20 points
  • 5th Place 10 points

We'll aggregate points from all 10 games to determine our champion. In case of a tie, fair play points will be considered as the tiebreaker. The highest-scoring team becomes our Mini QPL Champion!

Champion Determination

Points are cumulative across all events. Teams should strive for consistency across all games rather than focusing solely on their strongest events. Remember that fair play can make the difference in a close competition!

The Spirit of Fair Play

How you play matters more than whether you win

At the heart of Mini QPL lies the principle of fair play. This year, we're challenging the notion that fair play and competitive success are mutually exclusive. We want to see teams that can win both the trophy and the fair play award, proving that positive sportsmanship and excellence can go hand in hand.

Respect

Respect opponents, teammates, and officials always

Humility

Celebrate victories with humility and acknowledge everyone's efforts

Grace

Accept defeats gracefully, focusing on lessons learned

Support

Support and encourage less experienced players

Energy

Maintain positive energy throughout all events

Remember

As we prepare for Mini QPL, remember that while trophies gather dust, the relationships and wellbeing benefits we cultivate will continue to enrich our professional and personal lives long after the final whistle.

Carrom - Official Rules

A classic tabletop game requiring precision and strategy

Starting the Game

  • Winner of coin toss chooses to strike first OR select preferred board side
  • First player gets three attempts to execute valid break
  • Valid break requires striking central coin AND moving multiple coins/pocketing one

Basic Rules

  • Players pocket only their assigned color coins
  • Pocketing a coin earns one additional turn
  • Strike using only fingers/knuckles
  • Striker must be within baseline and quarter circle

Queen Rules

  • Must cover Queen (pocket Queen then pocket your color in next shot)
  • Queen worth 3 points when covered
  • Must pocket and cover Queen before your last coin
  • Cannot attempt Queen until pocketing at least one of your coins

Match Format: 5 games (2 men's singles, 1 women's singles, 1 mixed doubles, 1 men's doubles)

Cricket - Official Rules

8 Overs Format

Team Composition

  • 10 players per team (including 2 female players) + 2 substitutes
  • Each match consists of 8 overs
  • Minimum 5 bowlers per team (1 female bowler mandatory)
  • Maximum 2 overs per bowler
  • 1 over must be played by a female batter

Scoring

  • Runs scored by running between wickets
  • Boundary: 4 runs (hitting ground first) or 6 runs (direct)
  • Additional runs: Wide-ball, No-ball, Byes, Overthrows

Valid Dismissals

  • Bowled: Ball hits stumps, dislodging bails
  • Caught: Fielder catches ball before it touches ground or walls
  • Run Out: Fielder hits stumps while batsman is out of crease during run
  • Stumped: Wicketkeeper puts down stumps while batsman is out of crease (not attempting run)
  • Hit Wicket: Batsman dislodges bails with bat/body during shot or run
  • Mankading: After warning, non-striker leaves crease before delivery

Tournament-Specific Rules:

  • No LBW dismissals
  • No Leg Byes allowed
  • Byes and Overthrows count
  • Wides: Ball passing outside wide marker
  • No Ball: Above waist-height full toss
  • Bouncers: One per over allowed (shoulder to head height)
  • Free Hit awarded after any No Ball
  • Plastic stumps: Valid dismissal if any part of frame is hit

Chess - Official Rules

The ultimate battle of minds requiring strategic thinking and foresight

Format

  • 5 players per team (predetermined board order)
  • 5 minutes per player (10 minutes total per match)
  • Each win = 1 point; 3+ points to win match

Special Rules

  • If tied 2.5-2.5: Armageddon tiebreaker (White: 5 min, Black: 4 min; Black wins on draw)
  • No draw by repetition
  • Illegal moves: 10-second penalty to opponent
  • Pressing clock without moving: Two warnings, then 20-second penalty
  • Touch-and-move forgiven twice per player, enforced thereafter

Standard Rules Apply:

  • En Passant allowed
  • Normal castling restrictions apply
  • King must be touched first when castling
×

Pool (8-Ball) - Official Rules

A game of precision, strategy, and steady hands

Basic Play

  • Standard 8-Ball rules with teams lagging for first break
  • Legal break: Hit lead ball first and either pocket a ball or drive four balls to rails
  • Table open after break until a player legally pockets a ball
  • Players must hit their group balls (stripes/solids) first
  • Turn passes if no ball pocketed
  • 8-ball must be pocketed in called pocket to win
  • One foot must remain on floor during shot
  • 45-second time limit per shot

Fouls (Result in Ball-in-Hand)

  • Missing all balls with cue ball
  • Hitting opponent's balls first
  • Scratching (pocketing cue ball)
  • Driving any ball off table
  • No rail contact after hitting object ball
  • Double hitting/pushing cue ball
  • Illegal jump shots (no scooping)

Tournament Format: Singles, Mixed Doubles, Doubles

Table Tennis - Official Rules

Lightning-quick reflexes and precision are key in this exciting indoor sport

Format

  • 5 formats: 2 men's singles, 1 women's singles, 1 men's doubles, 1 mixed doubles
  • Each format: Best of 3 sets to 11 points (win by 2 in deuce)
  • Team must win 3 of 5 formats to advance

Key Rules

  • Serve alternates every 2 points (every 1 point at 10-10)
  • Servers must toss ball 6" up from open palm
  • Net serve is redone ("let")
  • Partners must alternate hits in doubles
  • No volleys (must let ball bounce)
  • Top edge hits are in; side edges are out
×

UNO - Official Rules

Fast-paced card game of matching colors and numbers that's fun for everyone

Basic Rules

  • 5 players per team
  • Deal 7 cards to each player
  • Play cards matching color/number/symbol of top discard card
  • Special cards: Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Draw Four, Wild
  • Can't play +2 on +4 or vice versa
  • Must call "UNO" with one card remaining
  • No special cards allowed for last card
  • First 3 players to clear all cards wins for their team
×

Volleyball - Official Rules

Exciting team sport that combines agility, coordination, and teamwork

Format

  • 6 players on court, 2 reserves
  • Best of 3 sets to 25 points (win by 2)

Key Rules

  • Maximum 3 hits per side
  • Cannot hit ball twice in succession (blocks don't count as hits)
  • No touching net during play
  • Ball on line is "in"
  • No player can serve consecutive turnovers
  • No rotation required
  • Standard volleyball rules otherwise apply

Note: Organizers'/referee's decision will be final for all games.

×

Badminton - Official Rules

Fast-paced racquet sport that tests reflexes and agility

Format

  • 5 matches total: 2 men's singles, 1 women's singles, 1 men's doubles, 1 mixed doubles
  • Each match: Best of 3 sets, 11 points per set (win by 2 in deuce)
  • Team must win 3 of 5 matches to win
  • Player lineup must be submitted in advance and cannot be changed
  • Default win if opponent lacks required players

Key Rules

  • Diagonal underarm serves only (below waist)
  • Singles: Use inside sidelines and back boundary lines
  • Doubles: Inner boundary for serves, full court after return
  • Serve from right court on even scores, left on odd scores
  • On-line shots are considered IN
  • Standard badminton rules apply for all other aspects
×

Football (7v7) - Official Rules

The beautiful game that combines athleticism and team strategy

Basic Rules

  • 7 players per team (including goalkeeper), 2 rolling substitutes allowed
  • 15-minute halves with 5-minute break
  • Kick-ins instead of throw-ins (opponents must be 3m away)
  • No offside rule
  • All free kicks direct unless back-pass to keeper
  • Standard rules for fouls and misconduct
×

Pickleball - Official Rules

Our exciting new addition combining elements of tennis, badminton, and ping-pong

Format

  • Each match includes singles, mixed doubles, and men's doubles
  • Teams need minimum 4 players (at least 1 female)
  • Sets to 11 points, win by 2 (best of 3 sets)
  • Win 2 of 3 games to win match

Key Rules

  • Diagonal underhand serves only
  • Double bounce rule: First returns from each side must bounce
  • No volleying in non-volley zone (kitchen)
  • Only serving team scores points
  • First serve of each side-out from right-hand court
  • Players serve entire turn from alternating sides
